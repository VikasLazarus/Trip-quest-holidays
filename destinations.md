<p>If you are planning your next getaway, here is a curated guide to help you explore popular destinations of Himachal Pradesh.</p>


<h2>Shimla & Kufri: Colonial Charm Meets Winter Magic</h2>
<p>The state capital remains one of the most visited hill stations in India, blending heritage architecture with modern amenities. Walking down the Mall Road or visiting the historic Gaiety Theatre are staples, but modern travelers are also seeking curated Shimla tour packages that go beyond the obvious.</p>

<p>Just a short drive away is Kufri, a haven for snow lovers. Beyond the winter sports, a classic Kufri horse ride up to Mahasu Peak remains a beloved activity for families, offering panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges.</p>

<h2>>Manali: The Adventure Capital of North India</h2>
<p>A Kullu Manali trip is a rite of passage for honeymooners, backpackers, and families alike. While Old Manali offers a laid-back cafe culture, Solang Valley and the Atal Tunnel routes draw heavy tourist traffic for a reason.</p>

<h3>Top Activities: Paragliding, river rafting, and skiing in the winter months.</h3>

<p>Why it's trending in 2026:iti Valley: The Cold Desert Wonderland</p>

<p>If you want to step away from the crowds, a Spiti Valley tour is the ultimate adventure. Known for its high-altitude desert landscapes, ancient Buddhist monasteries like Key Gompa, and unparalleled stargazing, Spiti has emerged as a dream road-trip destination.</p>

<p>Improved road conditions in 2026 have made it slightly more accessible, though it still demands respect and proper acclimatization. It is an absolute must-visit for bikers, photographers, and those looking to disconnect.</p>

<h2>Dharamshala & McLeod Ganj: The Spiritual Hub</h2>
<p>Famed as the residence of the Dalai Lama, Dharamshala and its upper suburb McLeod Ganj are witnessing a massive surge in wellness tourism. It is one of the best places to visit in Himachal if you want to combine spiritual growth with physical activity.</p>

<h2>Highlights: Tibetan culture, serene monasteries, and the famous Triund trek.</h2>

<h3>Tirthan & Jibhi: The Rising Stars of Eco-Tourism</h3>
<p>For those specifically looking for offbeat places in Himachal, Tirthan Valley and nearby Jibhi are the perfect escapes. Acting as the gateway to the Great Himalayan National Park (GHNP), this region is pristine, less commercialized, and heavily focused on eco-tourism and authentic homestays.</p>
